// methodology

From Field
to Genome

Our pipeline integrates classic microbiology with cutting-edge multi-omics approaches. From collecting water samples in volcanic craters to resolving population-level genomic variation — every step is designed to capture the full complexity of life in extreme geothermal environments.

1
Field Sampling
In-situ measurements, water and sediment collection across the full geothermal gradient. Physicochemical characterization on-site.
2
Isolation & Culture
Selective media, enrichment cultures, and strain isolation under acidic conditions. Growth characterization and preservation.
3
Metagenomics
Shotgun sequencing of community DNA. Assembly, binning, and MAG recovery to resolve community-level functional potential.
4
Genomics & Evolution
Whole genome sequencing, pan-genome analysis, population genomics, phylogenomics. Detecting selection signatures and adaptive genes.
5
Global Databases
Cross-referencing with NCBI, JGI/IMG, SILVA, and public AMD metagenomes to contextualize local findings at global scale.
AMD

Acid Mine Drainage & Global Context

The same organisms thriving in Copahue's volcanic acid pools are key drivers of acid mine drainage worldwide — a billion-dollar environmental challenge. Understanding their ecology, evolution, and metabolic capabilities has direct implications for bioremediation, biomining, and environmental monitoring at industrial scales.
