Our Work

Research

We study the evolution and ecology of acidophilic microorganisms across extreme environmental gradients.
Our research integrates molecular biology, bioinformatics, and field ecology to understand how microbial communities adapt to the most demanding conditions on Earth — from acid mine drainage to volcanic springs. We ask fundamental questions about evolutionary mechanisms, metabolic flexibility, and ecological resilience.
Research Lines
01

Microbial Evolution in Extreme Environments

Understanding how selective pressure shapes genomic architecture and metabolic pathways in acidophilic organisms along environmental gradients.

02

Community Ecology of Acid Mine Drainage

Characterizing microbial diversity and functional redundancy in AMD systems, and how community structure responds to geochemical variation.

03

Genomics and Comparative Genomics

Using whole-genome sequencing and comparative approaches to identify genes and pathways under positive selection in extreme-adapted lineages.

04

Biogeochemical Cycling

Exploring the role of extremophile communities in sulfur, iron, and carbon cycling, and their broader impact on ecosystem-level processes.
