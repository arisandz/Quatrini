Research

Overview

The Quatrini Lab investigates the biology, ecology, and evolution of microorganisms that thrive in extreme acidic environments — principally the acid mine drainage (AMD) systems of the Atacama and Andean regions of Chile. We combine comparative genomics, metagenomics, experimental evolution, and molecular microbiology to understand how life adapts to chemical extremes.

Our work sits at the interface of basic microbial science and applied biotechnology, with implications for bioleaching, bioremediation, and our broader understanding of the limits of life on Earth — and potentially beyond.

Research Themes
// Genomics

Comparative & Functional Genomics

We decode the genomic architecture of acidophilic bacteria to understand gene function, regulatory networks, and the molecular underpinnings of acid tolerance, metal resistance, and energy conservation in low-pH environments.

Pangenomics Gene Regulation Acidithiobacillia
// Ecology

Microbial Community Ecology in AMD

Acid mine drainage systems host simplified, tractable microbial communities. We reconstruct community composition, metabolic interactions, and ecological networks using metagenome-assembled genomes (MAGs) and multi-omics approaches.

Metagenomics MAGs Network Analysis
// Evolution

Experimental Evolution Under Extreme Stress

Laboratory evolution experiments expose acidophilic strains to escalating chemical stressors — chloride, metal ions, pH gradients — allowing us to directly observe adaptive trajectories, mutation spectra, and the repeatability of evolution.

Exp. Evolution Selection Pressure Adaptation
// Virology

Viral Ecology & Phage-Host Interactions

Bacteriophages shape the population structure and gene flow of acidophilic communities. We study phage diversity, lysogeny, and phage-host coevolution through metagenome-resolved viromics and experimental infection assays.

Viromics Phage Biology Coevolution
// Metabolism

Nitrogen & Sulfur Cycling Pathways

Biogeochemical cycling in AMD is dominated by sulfur and nitrogen transformations driven by acidophilic chemolithotrophs. We dissect the enzymes, operons, and regulatory circuits that govern these cycles at the molecular level.

N Metabolism S Cycling Chemolithotrophy
// Defense

Genome Defense & Mobile Genetic Elements

Restriction-modification systems, CRISPR arrays, and mobile genetic elements are key drivers of genome plasticity in acidophiles. We catalogue, characterize, and functionally dissect these systems to understand genome integrity and horizontal gene transfer.

RM Systems CRISPR HGT
Our Approaches
01

Multi-Omics Integration

We combine genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics data into unified models of cellular and community function — connecting genotype to phenotype across environmental gradients.

02

Field Sampling & Environmental Gradients

AMD sites in Chile's Atacama region provide natural experiments across pH, temperature, metal concentration, and salinity. We sample across these gradients to capture the full spectrum of microbial diversity.

03

Molecular Microbiology

Complementing computational work, we perform genetic manipulation, reporter assays, protein characterization, and growth experiments in tractable acidophilic model organisms to validate in silico predictions.

04

Computational Biology & AI-Assisted Analysis

Bioinformatic pipelines for MAG reconstruction, pangenome analysis, phylogenetics, and increasingly AI-assisted annotation and network inference are central to scaling our research questions.

Meet the Acidithiobacillia

The class Acidithiobacillia sits at the very heart of our research. These sulfur- and iron-oxidizing chemolithotrophs are the dominant architects of acid mine drainage ecosystems worldwide — and one of the most phylogenetically distinctive lineages of Bacteria.
