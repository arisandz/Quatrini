Research Lines

Science at the
Edge of Life

We study microorganisms thriving in extreme acidic environments — uncovering their ecology, evolution, and biotechnological potential.

Active Research Lines

01

Microbial Ecology

Community Dynamics in Acid Mine Drainage

Characterizing microbial community structure and function along physicochemical gradients in AMD systems using amplicon sequencing and metagenomics. We aim to understand the ecological drivers of community assembly in these low-pH, metal-rich habitats.

16S rRNA Metagenomics AMD Gradient Analysis
Active
02

Microbial Evolution

Adaptive Evolution of Acidophilic Microorganisms

Investigating the molecular mechanisms and evolutionary trajectories that allow microorganisms to thrive at extremely low pH. We use comparative genomics and experimental evolution approaches to identify key adaptive strategies.

Comparative Genomics Acidophiles Experimental Evolution
Active
03

Biogeochemistry

Microbially Driven Iron and Sulfur Cycling

Quantifying the contribution of acidophilic bacteria and archaea to iron oxidation and sulfur oxidation-reduction cycles in extreme environments. Linking metabolic potential from metagenomes to measured geochemical fluxes.

Iron Oxidation Sulfur Cycling MAGs Geochemistry
Ongoing
04

Biotechnology

Bioleaching and Biomining Applications

Translating fundamental knowledge of acidophile physiology into biotechnological applications, particularly for mineral bioleaching and sustainable metal recovery from low-grade ores.

Bioleaching Biomining Applied Microbiology
Ongoing
