Life at the edge of pH

Along the geothermal gradient of the Copahue–Caviahue–Chancoco system, we decode how extreme physicochemical conditions shape microbial communities — from species distribution to evolutionary adaptation, one acid pool at a time.

// methodology

From Field to Genome

Our pipeline integrates classic microbiology with multi-omics approaches — from collecting water samples in volcanic craters to resolving population-level genomic variation.

1
Field Sampling

In-situ measurements, water and sediment collection across the full geothermal gradient. Physicochemical characterization on-site.

2
Isolation & Culture

Selective media, enrichment cultures, and strain isolation under acidic conditions. Growth characterization and preservation.

3
Metagenomics

Shotgun sequencing of community DNA. Assembly, binning, and MAG recovery to resolve community-level functional potential.

4
Genomics & Evolution

Whole-genome sequencing, pan-genome analysis, population genomics, phylogenomics. Detecting selection signatures and adaptive genes.

5
Global Databases

Cross-referencing with NCBI, JGI/IMG, SILVA, and public AMD metagenomes to contextualize local findings at global scale.
